###### Note:

- Accompanying travellers will be covered as part of the Complimentary Travel Insurance provided for flights originating from Singapore.
- Only Singapore Residents are eligible for the Complimentary Travel Insurance. A Singapore Resident means a Singapore citizen, Singapore Permanent Resident, or foreigner holding a valid Work Permit, Employment Pass, Dependantâs Pass, Long\-Term Visit Pass or Student Pass.
- For travel insurance with higher coverage and additional benefits (such as coverage for non\-COVID\-19 related overseas medical expenses due to accident or sickness), you may wish to purchase [TravellerShield Plus](https://www.dbs.com.sg/personal/insurance/travel/travellershield-plus?pid=sg-dbs-pweb-support-text-travellershield-plus).

  

- **I just booked a flight via DBS Travel \& Leisure Marketplace. How long does the Complimentary Travel Insurance policy cover me?**  
  

**For flights booked via DBS Travel \& Leisure Marketplace**
	- If you are travelling on a return trip commencing from and ending in Singapore, your coverage starts on the scheduled departure date and ends on the scheduled arrival date, as indicated on your flight itinerary. However, the COVID\-19 Cover (Section 5\) is only applicable for up to 30 days, starting from your scheduled departure date.
	- If you are travelling on a one\-way trip, your coverage (across all benefits from Sections 1 to 7\) starts on the scheduled departure date from Singapore for up to 15 days.

- **Does my policy cover me for COVID\-19 related claims?**  
  

Yes, COVID\-19 Cover is available for all policies issued. It covers travel to all destinations\*, except Cuba. View all [COVID\-19 benefits](https://www.dbs.com.sg/iwov-resources/pdf/others/dbs-complimentary-travel-insurance-policy.pdf).

*\*Please note that this is subject to change, depending on the development of the COVID\-19 situation.*

- **Does the COVID\-19 Cover also include expenses related to COVID\-19 diagnostic tests?**  
  
 
 No, any expenses related to COVID\-19 diagnostic tests, including pre\-departure tests and post\-arrival tests, are not covered under this benefit.
