### Know your jargon

One of the first steps to buying a used car is to know your jargon. In the second\-hand car buying process, differentiating your PARF from your ARF is key:

- **Certificate of Entitlement (COE):** Legal document allowing a car to be driven on Singapore roads. Can be renewed for 5\- or 10\-year durations.
- **Open Market Value (OMV):** Every car has an Open Market Value, which is the price payable when a vehicle is imported into Singapore. It is determined by Singapore Customs based on the carГўВҖВҷs purchase price, freight, insurance and all other charges related to the sale and shipment of the car to Singapore. The OMV also determines your maximum car loan.
- **PARF (Preferential Additional Registration Fee):** Those keen on second\-hand cars will likely be looking at PARF cars which are less than 10 years old. Note that you may have difficulty in securing a loan for a COE car, which have had their COE renewed, and are usually more than 10 years old.
- **Additional Registration Fee (ARF):** Lastly, thereГўВҖВҷs ARF, a tiered tax that you pay when registering a car. It is based on the OMV of the car. All cars in Singapore are subject to ARF.

### Know your used car valuation factors

If browsing through all the makes, models and mileage history are making you blurry eyed, we suggest starting with the numbers. LetГўВҖВҷs look at some factors that might affect a used carГўВҖВҷs valuation.

**Mileage \*Most important!\***

Mileage refers to the total distance that a car has clocked, and is an indication of the amount of wear and tear that the car has sustained over time. Mileage is considered high or low relative to the car's age.

A totally new car is of course like a blank slate and has the lowest mileage. With used cars, look for something that has lower mileage (per year) than the [average 17,500km clocked by private cars in Singapore](https://www.dbs.com.sg/personal/common-disclaimer.page?url=https%3A%2F%2Fdata.gov.sg%2Fdataset%2Fannual-mileage-for-private-motor-vehicles&3rdparty=%27Data%20Gov%20SG%27&pid=sg-dbs-pweb-article-buying-used-car-textlink-datagovsg).

**Depreciation**

Cars are depreciating assets, which means that their value drops each year and youГўВҖВҷre unlikely to make a profit from selling your car.

Between new and used cars, new cars tend to depreciate faster than used cars, with the steepest drop in the first year alone.

Note: the [deregistration](https://www.dbs.com.sg/personal/common-disclaimer.page?url=https%3A%2F%2Fonemotoring.lta.gov.sg%2Fcontent%2Fonemotoring%2Fhome%2Fselling-deregistering%2Fderegister-a-vehicle.html&3rdparty=%27LTA%27&pid=sg-dbs-pweb-article-buying-used-car-textlink-deregistration) value (also known as ГўВҖВңpaper valueГўВҖВқ) is the sum of the COE rebate and PARF rebate.

**COE**

New or used car, youГўВҖВҷll still need to pay COE to drive on Singapore roads. Keep the above depreciation formula in mind as well, which is affected by COE. This could determine if specific second\-hand cars are more value\-for\-money than new ones.

**Age**

ItГўВҖВҷs said that a new car sees the most depreciation in the first four years of its life. However, a car that is too old might need a lot more trips to the car workshop. When buying a used car, a good age is between four to eight years old.

**Condition**

It goes without saying that the physical condition of the car affects a used carГўВҖВҷs valuation. Not just how it looks, but also how well itГўВҖВҷs maintained and how smoothly it drives.

### What other costs should you consider?

When evaluating how much a car will cost, there tends to be a focus on downpayment and monthly instalments but do factor in other costs like insurance and maintenance.

**Interest rate**

In Singapore, used car loan interest rates for second\-hand cars are on par with loans for new cars. In fact, used car loans from DBS have no loading fee and the minimum loan is just S$10,000\.

**Maintenance**

This depends on the state of your used car purchase. A cheaper but older, run\-down ride with plenty of mileage on it might rack up higher maintenance costs.

**Insurance**

Good news! Expect to pay lower insurance premiums for used cars in Singapore, mainly because parts for second\-hand cars are readily available and easier to replace. However, just like insurance for a new car, factors such as the driverГўВҖВҷs age, gender, years of driving and experience also matter.

In addition, donГўВҖВҷt just go for the cheapest insurance plan you see, as there may be exclusions or a lot of excess to pay. For example, used car owners can opt for third\-party only (TPO) insurance, but that only covers damages to other peopleГўВҖВҷs cars, not yours. ### Know where to buy a second\-hand car

In Singapore, you can buy a used car directly from the owners, or from a dealer. Going straight to the seller means you can cut out middlemen fees and commission, but sometimes the deal may be too good to be true. Buying your second\-hand car from an accredited dealer with trusted reviews helps protect you against lemons. They also offer a wider range of choices and proper paperwork.

### Know how to finance your used car

Two words: Cash or Loan. The three types of used car loan financing methods are: fixed interest rate financing, in\-house financing and balloon scheme financing.

Most drivers opt for **fixed interest rate financing** directly from a bank. With this type of financing, the interest rate doesnГўВҖВҷt change during the tenure of the loan. [Find out more about the DBS car loan](https://www.dbs.com.sg/personal/loans/car-loans/carloan?pid=sg-dbs-pweb-article-buying-used-car-textlink-carloan).

**In\-house financing** from the dealership might be viable if youГўВҖВҷre unable to get a bank loan and/or if the car is too old. However, the interest rate may be higher and could include admin fees.

**Balloon scheme financing** excludes the Preferential Additional Registration Fee (PARF) of your vehicle, lowering your monthly loan repayments but increasing your used car loan interest rates. There may also be conditions attached and penalties if you change your car halfway.

### Know how to check a used car before buying

Now that youГўВҖВҷve worked out your sums and shortlisted your future dream car, itГўВҖВҷs time to actually make the purchase. While buying a used car is more affordable than a new one, its condition could determine your future maintenance and servicing costs.

Here are some basic steps to do a car inspection:

### Tips on what more to ask and inspect

**From 10 steps away:** Do the body panels look straight? Is the car body leaning to one side? If anything doesnГўВҖВҷt look right, the car might have been in a prior accident with underlying damage ГўВҖВ” future repair costs for you! Conversely, if the defect is minor (e.g. dents in the door), you could highlight that to get a discount.

**From 5 steps away:** Check for missing trim pieces, inspect the paintwork and the rest of the exterior car body. For example, if the paint seems patchy, appears wavy in the light or shows visible scratches, that might indicate a prior accident.

**Open the bonnet:** Look out for oil leaks below the car and within the engine compartment. One sign is visible oily patches. Run the engine and listen closely for a ГўВҖВңchoppyГўВҖВқ sound. As the engine is the main component powering your future car, repairs to it or to the transmission can cost a lot.

**From inside:** While a clean and well\-maintained interior is nice to have, donГўВҖВҷt let that distract you from the key features ГўВҖВ” the seatbelts and electrical features. These are legitimate safety concerns for both yourself and your passengers.

**From the driverГўВҖВҷs seat:** Check the air\-conditioning and listen closely for any strange noises during acceleration, braking, or turning. When driving, does the car pull away cleanly or are the transmission shifts jerky? A newer car shouldnГўВҖВҷt feel like youГўВҖВҷre driving one with its COE renewed.

Although second\-hand cars are more affordable, theyГўВҖВҷre still a big\-ticket purchase, so do also ask detailed questions and request for additional paperwork. These include the used carГўВҖВҷs ownership history, maintenance records and modifications.

Also enquire if the seller is willing to cover current repairs needed, if the car has been in any accidents, and so on.
