When we were younger, many of us would have used cash, EZ\-Link cards, or ATM cards to pay for our purchases.

As we had limited access to other types of cards due to age and income restrictions, many of us may not be familiar with the differences between ATM and debit cards.

On the surface, they share some similarities. These include letting you withdraw money from an ATM and making cashless payments, with the utilised funds being deducted from a designated linked savings account. However, debit cards do provide other benefits over using an ATM card.

Here are **4 reasons** to consider using a debit card for your spending.

### 1\. **Online shopping friendly**

With the immense growth of online shopping and e\-commerce in recent years, you are very likely to be part of an ever\-growing crowd that has embraced the idea of online shopping being a click away, or transacting from the comfort of your own home.

In order for shoppers to enjoy this convenience, they will likely need to have a debit or credit card because most online retailers require it as a method of payment for transactions. Hence, if you are planning to do some online shopping while serving your National Service (NS), it is an opportune time to consider getting a debit card.

### 2\. **More benefits than ATM cards**

Like an ATM card, a debit card allows you to access ATM services and authorise NETS payments using your ATM PIN.

Above and beyond that, a debit card is supported by worldwide payment networks like Mastercard or Visa. This offers the benefit of payment security especially when making online or overseas purchases.

You will also be rewarded for your debit card spending with benefits like cashback and reward points, giving you bang for your buck.

These benefits are usually applicable to both your online and offline spending, allowing you to get rewarded whenever you choose to pay by your debit card.

When you use a credit card, you are essentially using money borrowed from the card issuing organisation or bank. On the other hand, spending charged to your debit card is deducted directly from your linked bank account.

This means that how much you can spend on your debit card is limited by the balance in your bank account, reducing the risk of uncontrolled spending. Youâre more likely to think twice about spending on a âwantâ.

With these conveniences in mind, a debit card makes for a worthy alternative to a credit card.
